Intro:

The covid pandemic has had a massive impact on our lives. The effects of this are also felt in energy markets where global primary energy consumption fell by 4.5% and carbon emissions fall by 6.3% in the largest year-to-year decline in over 50 years. The issue of oil from Russia, has also become a problem recently as well. All the while the world aims to be moving towards greener more renewable energy sources. With our visualizations we want to show what sources the power we are consuming to generate our power and to show features and historic trends between countries.

1. Worldwide Power Consumption Trends

Lets start off by taking a look at the big picture. In this case that is our entire total global power consumption. As the population of the planet grows and technology advances, there is a clear need for more energy resulting in a steady increase in consumption as well. In 2020, largely as a result of the covid pandemic was the first year in a while that global energy consumption has fallen (by almost 5%). This decline was mostly due to the drop in demand for oil which dipped 15 EJ (almost 9% since the previous year). With the current ongoing war in Ukraine and Oil being a hot topic I belive this trend will continue as well. Despite this dip in consumption, renewable and hydro energy remained unaffected and with renewables alone tripling over the course of the last 10 years. Still, it remains a rather small proportion of total energy consumption but with the world becoming more climate active and various climate agreements I hope it can also continue in the future.

Global Energy Consumption - Individual Sources

Next, we can take a closer look and explore the individual regions & countries. Historically the US was the leading energy consumer until they were overtaken by China in 2009 who have led the world since then. India has also been steadily climbing the ranks in the meantime as well. A clear trend that is visible on the visualizations below is that more developed nations also have higher energy consumption. This obviously makes a lot of sense. However it did not mean that they were using higher proportions of cleaner energy sources. In fact while the general overall trend is that renewable energy consumption is increasing, only around half of the highest 25 consumers in 2020 consume more than 10% of clean (hydro or renewable) energy. The leaders in that regard are Brazil with over 40% of their power coming from clean sources (most of which is from hydro) and then Canada with almost 30%.

Carbon Dioxide emission on a global scale

Carbon Dioxide is the gas emitted by humans. This includes gasses like methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases. These gasses get trapped in the atmosphere, causing global warming. As most people know the world is collectively trying to reduce Carbon Dioxide emissions to slow down the impact it has on global warming. Gas flaring is the practice of burning gas associated with oil extraction. this has been done since the beginning of oil production. This process is very wasteful and polluting to the environment but the major reason why we still do gas flaring is that it's relatively safe and the export of gas requires an economically viable market as the company doing the oil production needs to capture, transport, process, and sell the gas.

As we can see from the charts below on a World scale Carbon Dioxide emissions didn't really drop down over the last 20 years. We saw a drop in natural gas and total flaring in the 1980s but not from non-flaring emissions. And after the drop to 195 million tonnes of carbon dioxide, we saw an increase to 300 million tonnes of carbon dioxide in the year 2020. source: https://www.nytimes.com/guides/year-of-living-better/how-to-reduce-your-carbon-footprint source: https://www.worldbank.org/en/programs/gasflaringreduction/gas-flaring-explained

Carbon Dioxide Emissions on a per country bases

If we now look at a per country visualization we can observe that all south/central American countries have a slight increase in carbon dioxide emissions while we saw the production in most North American countries double and we saw a decrease in most European countries. Carbon dioxide production filtered by year

As we filter the same data on a per year bases we can observe that the carbon dioxide emission has stabilized in recent years. In the 1970's we observe that no western countries are in the list of top 10 list of countries that produce carbon dioxide emissions from flaring while in 2020 we see the United States of America as the 4th largest producer and we see China coming up in the top 10 of carbon dioxide emission production.

You can filter the data by clicking on a year you want to select. By filtering, you show the data from all countries in that year ordered by the amount of carbon dioxide emission production.
